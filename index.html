Our Philosophy

Three things we never rush.

The Fire

Everything on the grill touches oak and cherrywood embers before it touches a plate. No gas, no shortcuts — just heat that takes hours to earn.

The Age

Beef dry-aged a minimum of 35 days in our on-site cellar. The wine list favors the same patience: old vines, older vintages, poured by people who know why.

The Hour

Dining rooms face west. We seat the last tables of the evening so the room fills with the same amber light the restaurant is named for.

Head of the Kitchen

Rosalind Vance

Rosalind trained in Lyon before spending six years running live-fire kitchens across the Basque coast. She opened AMBRE to build the restaurant she always wanted to eat in: unpretentious ingredients, treated with real patience.

Her team butchers whole animals in-house, keeps a working relationship with three regional farms, and changes the menu with the seasons rather than the calendar.

"A good fire asks for nothing but time. That's the whole recipe."
